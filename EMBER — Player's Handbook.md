# EMBER — Player's Handbook

*Everything you need to **play** EMBER, in one place. This is the player-facing companion to `EMBER — Core Engine.md` — you can read this to learn the game without wading through the instructions meant for the AI Game Master.*

*(You don't paste this file to the AI — paste the Core Engine and a Genre Pack for that. This handbook is for **you**.)*

---

## What is EMBER?

EMBER is a solo roleplaying game where an **AI is your Game Master** and you are the hero of the story. The AI describes the world, plays everyone in it, and decides what happens; **you** decide what your character does. It works in any genre — fantasy, horror, sci-fi, apocalypse, or a world you invent together.

**What you need:** two six-sided dice (**2d6**) — real dice or a dice app. That's it. **You roll all the dice**; the AI never rolls.

## How a game feels

Play is a conversation with a rhythm:

1. **The AI sets the scene** and asks, in effect, *"What do you do?"*
2. **You say what your character does** — in plain language, however you like.
3. If the outcome is **uncertain and failure would be interesting**, the AI asks you to **roll**. Otherwise it just tells you what happens.
4. **You roll 2d6, add a stat, and report the total.** The AI narrates the result and asks what's next.

You're never picking from a menu of buttons — you just describe what you want to do, and the AI figures out which rule applies.

## Your five stats

Every character has five stats, rated from **-1 to +3**. They mean the same thing in every genre; only the flavor changes.

| Stat | What it's for |
|---|---|
| **Might** | Force, endurance, physical power |
| **Grace** | Agility, finesse, stealth, aim |
| **Wits** | Perception, intellect, knowledge, know-how |
| **Presence** | Charm, command, influence over others |
| **Resolve** | Composure, willpower, grit (and *sanity*, in horror) |

## Rolling the dice

When the AI calls for a roll, roll **2d6 and add the relevant stat** (plus or minus 1 if you have an advantage or disadvantage). Read the total:

- **10 or higher — Full success.** You do it, cleanly.
- **7 to 9 — Success at a cost.** You do it, but there's a complication, a price, a hard choice, or only partial progress.
- **6 or lower — Miss.** It goes wrong, and the AI throws something at you. **Bright side:** you **mark 1 XP** — failure is how your character grows.

That middle band (7–9) is the soul of EMBER. Most of what you attempt will work — the drama is in what it *costs* you.

**No difficulty numbers.** You never chase a secret target number. The AI just gives you **+1 (advantage)** when things favor you, or **−1 (disadvantage)** when they don't.

## Moves — the things you do

"Moves" are just the common actions that trigger a roll. You don't announce them — you describe your action, and the AI applies the matching move. There are six basic ones:

- **Face Danger** — act under pressure or risk (any stat, depending on *how* you act).
- **Strike** — attack an enemy (Might for melee/force, Grace for finesse/ranged).
- **Endure** — resist harm, fear, or exhaustion (Might for body, Resolve for mind).
- **Read** — study a person, place, or situation to learn about it (Wits). On a hit you get to **ask the AI questions** and act on the answers for +1.
- **Sway** — influence someone with charm, logic, or intimidation (Presence).
- **Recover** — treat wounds, rest, or steady yourself when you get a safe moment.

Your **archetype** (see below) gives you extra **signature moves** on top of these — special things only your kind of character can do.

## Tags & pushing yourself

**Tags** are short descriptors that are true about you, your gear, or your surroundings: *Ex-soldier, Silver-tongued, Enchanted blade, High ground.* When a tag clearly applies to what you're doing, you get **+1**. Your gear is just tags — a *Plasma cutter* or a *Loyal hound* helps when it makes sense.

**Push yourself:** when you really want an edge, you can **take a Condition** (see below) to get **+1** on a roll. You're straining past your limits — and paying for it. There are no luck points or hero tokens; if you want more power, you risk more.

## Getting hurt: Harm, Conditions & Scars

- **Harm track:** you have **5 boxes**. Injuries fill them. Fill all five and you're **Taken Out** — out of the scene, and the AI decides the consequence (which isn't always death). Harm heals with rest and the **Recover** move, and resets between adventures.
- **Conditions:** named bad states like *Shaken, Wounded, Afraid, Compromised.* Each gives you **−1** on rolls it affects until you clear it.
- **Scars:** a really dramatic wound can leave a lasting mark. Some are just flavor; worse ones give you a small penalty for a while (a limp, trembling hands) — anywhere from the rest of the session to permanently, depending on how bad it was. Scars are rare and memorable, not routine.

You'll also sometimes see the AI track a **Clock** — a row of boxes it fills as danger builds (a rising flood, an approaching horde, a ritual nearing completion). When it fills, something happens. Clocks are your cue that the pressure is on.

## Making your character

Five quick steps (about five minutes) — or grab a ready-made **pre-gen** from your Genre Pack and skip straight to playing.

1. **Concept.** One sentence: *"a disgraced knight hunting the thing that killed her squad."*
2. **Archetype.** Pick one from your genre's list (e.g. Warrior, Mage, Rogue in fantasy). It gives you 1–2 signature moves and suggests which stats to favor.
3. **Stats.** Arrange these five numbers across your stats however fits your concept: **+2, +1, +1, 0, −1.** (Some gritty settings hand you a slightly weaker spread — the AI will say so.)
4. **Tags.** Pick **three** — your background, a skill, and your signature gear. Each gives +1 when it applies.
5. **Drive & Bond.** Write one **Drive** (a goal — pursuing it earns XP) and one **Bond** (a connection to a person or the world).

You start healthy: empty Harm track, no Conditions. Your character is stamped with its **genre** — it can play any *flavor* of that genre, but a fantasy hero can't hop into a spaceship (see below).

## Getting better: XP & advancement

You **mark 1 XP** when you **miss** a roll, **pursue your Drive**, or **deepen a Bond.** Spend XP between scenes:

| Cost | What you get |
|---|---|
| **5 XP** | A new **tag** (a skill or trait you've earned) |
| **5 XP** | A new **signature move** from your archetype |
| **8 XP** | **+1 to a stat** (maximum +3) |
| **8 XP** | **Harden** — shrug off a little Harm once per scene |

Your growth should reflect what you actually did in the story, not just spending points.

## Your character sticks around

- **Your character is saved to a file** and carries forward between adventures — your XP, learned moves, earned gear, and reputation all come with you (within the same genre). Wounds heal between adventures, but big Scars and your **Legacy log** of deeds travel with you.
- **The AI saves your progress automatically** as you play, so a single adventure can span many sessions. Stop whenever you like — when you come back, the AI reloads exactly where you left off. You never have to remember to save.
- **Genre lock:** a character belongs to one genre. They can explore every *lens* of it (a fantasy hero can go from high-magic to gritty sword-and-sorcery), but they can't cross into a totally different genre.

## Quick tips for great play

- **Describe, don't optimize.** Say what your character *does* and *why* — the vivid stuff gives the AI more to work with.
- **Lean into the 7–9s.** "Success at a cost" moments are where the best stories happen. Embrace the complication.
- **Push when it matters.** Taking a Condition for +1 at a dramatic moment is often worth it.
- **Use your tags out loud.** If you think something about you or your gear should help, say so — the AI will usually grant the +1.
- **Missing is okay.** A 6− hands you XP and hands the AI a plot twist. Failure moves the story; it doesn't end it.
