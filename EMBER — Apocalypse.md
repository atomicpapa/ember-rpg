# EMBER — Apocalypse

*A Genre Pack for EMBER. Paste this together with `EMBER — Core Engine.md`. Uses only Core rules — no new subsystems.*

**Tone & premise:** The world ended; people didn't. Survivors scratch a living from the ruins, band into fragile communities, and face the dead, the desperate, and the wasteland itself. **Shared spine:** scarcity, survival, factions, a broken world, and scavenging — every lens of Apocalypse shares this.

**Default dials:** Lethality 3 · Magic/Tech 1 · Weirdness 1. Grittier starting array (**+2, +1, 0, 0, −1**). Resources are scarce — treat ammo, fuel, medicine, and clean water as consumable tags that run out.

**Running Apocalypse well:** scarcity is the engine of drama — make the player choose what to spend and what to leave behind. Use Clocks for pursuit, dwindling supplies, spreading danger, and the horde at the gate. Communities and NPCs matter; a Bond to a settlement or a person gives the ruin stakes. Scars come easily here (Lethality 3) — the wasteland marks everyone.

## Archetype menu

Each archetype lists a stat emphasis and signature moves; new characters begin with the **starred** move.

**Raider** — *emphasize Might.*
- ★ **Take What You Need.** When you fight or intimidate to seize something, on a hit it's yours; on a 10+ without making a lasting enemy of everyone watching. On a miss, you get it *and* a reputation that follows you.
- **Hard to Kill.** Once per session, when you'd be Taken Out, instead drop to your last Harm segment and keep fighting.

**Medic** — *emphasize Wits.*
- ★ **Field Medicine.** When you treat wounds with real supplies, on a 10+ clear Harm or a Condition cleanly; on 7–9 it works but costs precious supplies or time. On a miss, it's worse than you thought.
- **Triage.** When you decide who to save under pressure, ask the GM who's most savable and what saving them will cost.

**Scavenger** — *emphasize Grace or Wits.*
- ★ **Sift the Ruins.** When you search a dangerous place for supplies, on a hit you find something useful; on a 10+ you also find it before the danger finds you. On a miss, you find it — and trouble.
- **Light Feet.** When you move through hazardous terrain quietly or quickly, take +1.

**Fixer** — *emphasize Wits.*
- ★ **Make It Work.** When you repair, jury-rig, or craft from scrap, on a 10+ it works; on 7–9 it works but is fragile, loud, or eats a scarce part. On a miss, you've wasted the parts.
- **Barter.** When you trade goods or labor, on a hit you get a fair deal or better.

**Wanderer** — *emphasize Grace or Resolve.*
- ★ **Road-Wise.** When you navigate the wastes or read the signs of danger ahead, on a hit you find the safer path; on a 10+ you also know what's coming. 
- **Lone Survivor.** When you Endure hardship alone, on a 7–9 take no Condition (still any Harm).

**Leader** — *emphasize Presence.*
- ★ **Rally.** When you inspire people to hold together in a crisis, on a hit they do; on a 10+ one of them steps up and aids you (+1) next roll.
- **Hard Choices.** Once per session, when you make a ruthless call for the group's survival, treat a related 6− as a 7–9 — the weight is on you, but it works.

## Threat roster

> **Scavenger Dogs (Minor, 1 Harm each; run in packs)** — Tags: *Fast, Starving, Pack.* Signature: *On your miss, the pack drags you down — take 1 Harm and Compromised.* Instinct: *Eat, or be eaten.*

> **Desperate Survivors (Standard, 3 Harm)** — Tags: *Cornered, Improvised weapons, Have families to feed.* Signature: *On your cost, they don't want to fight — but they will for what you're carrying.* Instinct: *Survive one more day, whatever it takes.*

> **Raider Gang (Standard, 3 Harm)** — Tags: *Cruel, Numbers, Scrap-armored.* Signature: *On your miss, they take a hostage or your supplies — tick a "They're winning" Clock.* Instinct: *Take, break, and rule through fear.*

> **The Horde (Elite, 5 Harm — a mass, not one thing)** — Tags: *Endless, Draws more with noise, No fear.* Signature: *Every loud action ticks the "Horde closes in" Clock; when it fills, you're surrounded.* Instinct: *Consume everything in its path.*

> **Warlord's Champion (Elite, 5 Harm)** — Tags: *Brutal, Trophy armor, Feared.* Signature: *A full blow fills 2–3 Harm; beating them in front of their people shakes the whole gang.* Instinct: *Prove strength; crush challengers.*

> **The Warlord (Boss, 5 Harm + 6-Clock "The Convoy Rolls")** — Tags: *Commands an army, Armed vehicles, Charismatic tyrant.* Signature: *Fights with numbers and machines, not fairly; on your miss, tick The Convoy Rolls. When it fills, they overrun the settlement you're defending.* Instinct: *Expand the empire; break anyone who resists.*

## Lenses

**Wasteland** *(signature — default dials).* Mad Max / road-warrior: scarce fuel, armored vehicles, warlords and convoys, brutal freedom under a burning sky. *Dials: Lethality 3, Magic/Tech 1.* Vehicles and fuel are central; use chase Clocks; the Warlord roster shines.

**Undead.** Zombie outbreak: the dead walk, noise draws them, every bite is a countdown. *Dials: Lethality 3, Weirdness 2.* The Horde is the constant pressure; a bite is a Scar-or-worse; safety is always temporary. Trust between survivors is the real drama.

**Nuclear.** Fallout and mutation: radiation, ruined cities, mutants, and vaults of the old world. *Dials: Lethality 2, Magic/Tech 2, Weirdness 2.* Track radiation as a creeping Clock/Condition; a Geiger counter is a lifesaver; mutants join the roster.

**Plague.** Contagion and quarantine: an unseen killer, checkpoints, the hard math of who to trust and who to turn away. *Dials: Lethality 2, Weirdness 1.* Infection is a slow Clock; the worst threats are often other frightened people; masks and medicine are gold.
