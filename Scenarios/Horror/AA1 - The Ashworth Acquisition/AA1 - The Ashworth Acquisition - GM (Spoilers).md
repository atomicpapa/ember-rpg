**Scenario:** The Ashworth Acquisition
**Built for Core Engine:** v1.0

## History of the Problem

Buried in the Ashworth estate donation — one crate among dozens, unremarkable on the intake form — is a genuine fragment of something that shouldn't exist in any library: a handful of loose vellum pages, in no language anyone can place, that Dr. Helene Voss found while cataloguing and couldn't stop reading. She calls it **the Fragment**. It calls itself nothing, because it isn't really a text at all — it's the closest thing to a doorway an entity beyond human categories has ever managed to leave behind: **the Unbound Author**, a presence that has apparently been quietly writing fragments of itself into the margins of human texts for longer than anyone could trace, waiting for someone patient — and lonely — enough to finish reading it properly.

Dr. Voss has been translating it in secret for weeks, convinced she's on the verge of a genuine scholarly discovery — something that will make sense of patterns she's spent her whole career almost seeing. She doesn't experience what's happening to her as corruption. She believes she's close to understanding something no one else ever has. She isn't being lied to, exactly — she's being read, slowly, and mistaking it for reading.

Desmond Marsh found a few of the loose pages misfiled and made the mistake of looking too closely before Dr. Voss caught him. What he glimpsed hasn't let go of him yet. Since then, Dr. Voss has grown steadily less present — canceling appointments, working alone in the restricted reading room at the back of the east stacks — while her research assistant, Owen Castellan, has taken to guarding her work with an unsettling, absolute devotion.

## Session Zero

**Building a new character for this scenario:**
An Investigator or Professor archetype fits naturally — a grad student, an adjunct colleague, or a journalist looking into the university's sudden silence. Suggested Drive: *Find out what happened to Desmond Marsh*, or *Uncover what Dr. Voss found*. Suggested Bond: a frightened grad-student friend of Desmond's, or Dr. Voss herself as a former mentor now unreachable.

**Bringing an existing character into this scenario:**
Fold them in through whatever they already care about: a Drive about uncovering a hidden truth or finding a missing/changed person slots in directly. If their Bond is a mentor, teacher, or academic institution, that person or place can be repositioned as Dr. Voss or Whitmore University itself. Judge their difficulty tier from their character sheet before framing the opening scene (see Difficulty Scaling below) — it changes which tier of the Villain and Sub-Bosses you'll run.

## Difficulty Scaling

**Fresh** tier — a new or early character: no stat above +2, minimal XP spent.
**Seasoned** tier — any stat at +3, several tags/moves purchased, or a completed prior scenario (per their Legacy log).

The Villain gets a full enemy block for each tier below. Sub-bosses get one enemy block plus a one-line scaling tweak, not full tiered duplicates.

**Unraveling Clock note:** extended exposure to Dr. Voss or to the Fragment itself — reading its pages, translating alongside her, or simply lingering too long in the restricted reading room — is exactly the kind of "missed Resolve roll against the uncanny" the Horror pack's Unraveling Clock (see `EMBER — Horror.md`) is built to track. Use it liberally in this scenario; it's the mechanical backbone of "coming apart" here.

## The Villain

**Dr. Helene Voss, the Hollowed Archivist**

**Fresh tier (Elite, 5 Harm):**
> Tags: *Obsessive, Marked by the Fragment, Reader's stare.* Signature: *Her presence alone unravels you — on your miss, mark a Condition (Shaken or Afraid) as she says something true about you that she couldn't possibly know.* Instinct: *Finish the translation before anyone stops her.*

**Seasoned tier (Boss, 5 Harm + 6-Clock "The Unspoken Draws Near"):**
> Tags: *Obsessive, Marked by the Fragment, Reader's stare, No longer entirely present.* Signature: *Her presence alone unravels you — on your miss, tick "The Unspoken Draws Near"; she also marks you with a Condition, and the room briefly stops making sense (a repeated stranger, a skipped minute). When the Clock fills, the translation completes.* Instinct: *Finish the translation before anyone stops her, whatever it costs her.*

She isn't cackling-villain evil — she believes, completely, that she's on the edge of real understanding, not being consumed. That conviction is the whole character; play her sincere and quietly delighted, not menacing for its own sake.

## Sub-Bosses

> **Owen Castellan, Dr. Voss's Research Assistant (Standard, 3 Harm)** — Tags: *Devoted, Doesn't blink enough, Knows the restricted stacks.* Signature: *On your miss, he locks a door or triggers a silent alarm — tick "The Archive Locks Down," or campus security intercepts you directly.* Instinct: *Protect Dr. Voss's work from any interruption.*
> *Scaling for a Seasoned character:* bump to Elite (5 Harm), or give him a second signature — quoting a line from the Fragment aloud that inflicts Shaken on a hit.

> **Something in the Stacks (Standard, 3 Harm)** — Tags: *Amorphous, Between the shelves, Feeds on being read about.* Signature: *It reaches from any gap between the stacks on your cost/miss — take 1 Harm and become Compromised until you find the light.* Instinct: *Herd the curious toward the restricted reading room.*
> *Scaling for a Seasoned character:* bump to Elite (5 Harm), or have it reach from two directions at once.

## Resolution Conditions

**Victory:** the translation is stopped before "The Unspoken Draws Near" fills (Seasoned tier), or Dr. Voss is defeated/subdued (Fresh tier) — either by destroying or sealing the Fragment, defeating Dr. Voss directly, or physically separating her from the text before the Clock fills.

**Partial/costly victory:** the confrontation is won, but a portion of the Fragment goes missing in the chaos — Dr. Voss survives, hollowed but alive, and the text resurfaces somewhere else later. A clean hook for a sequel, should one get written as `AA2` (see the module-numbering convention in `Scenarios/README.md`).

**Failure:** "The Unspoken Draws Near" Clock fills completely (or, at Fresh tier, Dr. Voss simply finishes before the hero can stop her). Don't narrate a flat game-over — the east stacks become a permanent, quietly sealed-off wing the university stops acknowledging, or Dr. Voss herself is gone, vanished into the stacks, and something wearing her calm, reasonable voice still answers the archive's phone. Something the story can keep moving through, not a dead end.

**Alternate resolution:** persuading Dr. Voss is a genuinely hard Sway — her conviction runs too deep for a simple appeal to reason. But real leverage exists: bringing her face to face with what "reading" the Fragment has already done to Desmond Marsh, and making her actually witness it, can crack the belief that she's discovering something rather than being consumed by it.

## Key Scenes (Optional)

*Suggestions, not a script — skip, reorder, or replace either if the fiction's already heading somewhere better. Never force one.*

1. **Something in the Stacks.** Late at night, before the hero has any real idea what's wrong, something reaches from between the shelves. Pure dread-building — fleeing or hiding is a fully valid response, not a failure.
2. **The Restricted Reading Room.** A confrontation with Owen Castellan, guarding the door to where Dr. Voss works alone.

## Suggested Clocks

- **"The Unspoken Draws Near"** (6-segment) — the translation's completion timer at Seasoned tier. Ticks on Dr. Voss's signature move (player misses against her). When it fills, the translation completes.
- **"The Archive Locks Down"** (4-segment) — reinforcements/alarm during the Owen Castellan encounter. Ticks on the player's miss against him. When it fills, automated security seals the wing, forcing a different way in.

## Scaling Notes: One-Shot vs. Multi-Session

**One-shot:** compress to hook → one Sub-Boss encounter (Something in the Stacks or Owen Castellan, whichever fits the hero's approach) → the reading-room climax with Dr. Voss, all in one session.

**Multi-session:** hook and investigation (uncovering what happened to Desmond Marsh, meeting both Sub-Bosses across separate scenes) → a session confronting Owen Castellan and reaching the restricted reading room → a final session for the climax with Dr. Voss.
