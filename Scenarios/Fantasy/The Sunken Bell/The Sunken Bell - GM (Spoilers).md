**Scenario:** The Sunken Bell

## History of the Problem

The watch-tower that sank two generations ago didn't fall to a storm alone — the founding priest of Graybell Cove, driven mad by a season of drownings he couldn't stop, tried to bargain with the bay itself. He and his bell went under together, and the town told itself it was an accident.

He didn't drown. Or didn't stay drowned. Now calling himself the **Drowned Cantor**, he leads a small, devoted cult — the **Tideborn Choir** — who believe that finishing his bargain will finally end the drownings for good: wake the ancient thing beneath the bay (they call it **Fathoms-Deep**) and trade the town's safety for its... attention. In truth, waking it would drown Graybell Cove entirely, not save it. The Cantor believes otherwise, fully and fanatically.

The missing fishermen weren't random — the Choir has been taking them, one by one, and turning them into **tide-touched thralls** to help recover the sunken bell's clapper, the ritual's last missing piece. The ritual completes at the next spring tide, six days out, when the water is lowest and the tower's stump is exposed longest.

## Session Zero

**Building a new character for this scenario:**
A Warrior or Ranger with coastal roots fits naturally — a former sailor, a fisherman's kin, someone who already has a reason to be out on the water at odd hours. Suggested Drive: *Find out what's taking the fishermen*, or something more personal tied to one of the missing. Suggested Bond: Old Pell Ashgrove (the harbor-master) or a missing fisherman's sibling.

**Bringing an existing character into this scenario:**
Fold them in through whatever they already care about: if their Drive involves clearing a name or finding someone, one of the missing fishermen (or a witness) can hold a thread relevant to that. If their Bond is a specific person, that person can have moved to Graybell Cove, or simply be one of the missing. Judge their difficulty tier from their character sheet before framing the opening scene (see Difficulty Scaling below) — it changes which tier of the Villain and Sub-Bosses you'll run.

## Difficulty Scaling

**Fresh** tier — a new or early character: no stat above +2, minimal XP spent.
**Seasoned** tier — any stat at +3, several tags/moves purchased, or a completed prior scenario (per their Legacy log).

The Villain gets a full enemy block for each tier below. Sub-bosses get one enemy block plus a one-line scaling tweak, not full tiered duplicates.

## The Villain

**The Drowned Cantor**

**Fresh tier (Elite, 5 Harm):**
> Tags: *Zealous, Tide-blessed, Ritual dagger.* Signature: *Calls the tide itself — on your miss, a surge of water knocks you down (fills 1 Harm) or douses your light source.* Instinct: *Complete the ritual before the tide turns.*

**Seasoned tier (Boss, 5 Harm + 6-Clock "The Tide Turns"):**
> Tags: *Zealous, Tide-blessed, Ritual dagger, Speaks with a drowned voice.* Signature: *Calls the tide itself — on your miss, tick "The Tide Turns"; a surge of water also fills 2 Harm and knocks you Compromised. When the Clock fills, the ritual completes.* Instinct: *Complete the ritual before the tide turns, whatever the cost.*

He isn't cackling-villain evil — he believes, completely, that he's saving the town the only way left to him. That conviction is the whole character; play him sincere, not moustache-twirling.

## Sub-Bosses

> **Tide-touched Thralls (Standard, 3 Harm)** — Tags: *Drowned eyes, Unnaturally strong, Don't need to breathe.* Signature: *They drag you toward the water on your cost/miss — take 1 Harm and become Compromised until you break free.* Instinct: *Drag the unwilling into the tide.*
> *Scaling for a Seasoned character:* bump to Elite (5 Harm), or run two Thralls instead of one.

> **Brother Wrack, the Choir's Enforcer (Standard, 3 Harm)** — Tags: *Fanatical, Numbers, Hooked gaff.* Signature: *On your miss, he calls for backup — tick a 4-segment "The Choir Gathers" Clock, or another cultist arrives immediately.* Instinct: *Protect the Cantor's ritual at any cost.*
> *Scaling for a Seasoned character:* bump to Elite (5 Harm), or give him a second signature — a thrown net that fills 1 Harm and inflicts Compromised on a hit.

## Resolution Conditions

**Victory:** the ritual is stopped before "The Tide Turns" fills (Seasoned tier) or the Cantor is defeated/driven off (Fresh tier) — either by breaking the recovered bell-clapper, defeating the Cantor directly, or getting the town evacuated and physically disrupting the ritual site before the tide turns.

**Partial/costly victory:** the fight is won, but the Cantor escapes into the bay to try again elsewhere — a clean hook for a sequel, should one get written (see the Series convention in `Scenarios/README.md` if this becomes `SB2`).

**Failure:** "The Tide Turns" Clock fills completely (or, at Fresh tier, the Cantor simply succeeds because the hero couldn't stop him in time). Don't narrate a flat game-over — Fathoms-Deep's presence floods the low quarter of Graybell Cove permanently, transforming the town and creating a new standing threat or mystery. Something the story can keep moving through, not a dead end.

**Alternate resolution:** persuading the Cantor is a genuinely hard Sway (his fanaticism runs deep) — but real leverage exists: one of the tide-touched thralls, if freed and lucid for a moment, could be someone the Cantor still loves (a sibling, an old parishioner), and that recognition could break his resolve without a fight.

## Key Scenes (Optional)

*Suggestions, not a script — skip, reorder, or replace either if the fiction's already heading somewhere better. Never force one.*

1. **The tide-touched procession.** At dusk, the hero spots a small procession of tide-touched thralls carrying something wrapped in kelp and rope down to the shore — the recovered bell-clapper. A chance to intercept them here, or follow them back to find the ritual site.
2. **The ritual at the sunken bell.** The climax: at low tide, with the old watch-tower's stump exposed for the first time in years, the hero arrives to find the Cantor mid-ritual, thralls and Brother Wrack between them and him.

## Suggested Clocks

- **"The Tide Turns"** (6-segment) — the ritual's completion timer at Seasoned tier. Ticks on the Cantor's signature move (player misses against him). When it fills, the ritual completes.
- **"The Choir Gathers"** (4-segment) — reinforcements/alarm during the Brother Wrack encounter. Ticks on the player's miss against him. When it fills, more cultists arrive.

## Scaling Notes: One-Shot vs. Multi-Session

**One-shot:** compress to hook → one Sub-Boss encounter (Thralls or Brother Wrack, whichever fits the hero's approach) → the ritual climax with the Cantor, all in one session.

**Multi-session:** hook and investigation (uncovering the Choir, meeting both Sub-Bosses across separate scenes) → a session confronting Brother Wrack and the Choir directly → a final session for the ritual climax with the Cantor at the spring tide.
