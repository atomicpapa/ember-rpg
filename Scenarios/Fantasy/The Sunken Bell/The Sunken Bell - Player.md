**Scenario:** The Sunken Bell
**Genre / Suggested Lens:** Fantasy / any lens, leans naturally toward High Magic or Sword & Sorcery

## Premise

Graybell Cove has always lived with the tide, but lately the tide doesn't feel like it's living with Graybell Cove. Three fishermen have vanished in the past month, always at high tide, always without a trace — no wreckage, no bodies, nothing but their boats found empty and drifting. Old-timers swear they've heard a bell tolling from somewhere beneath the waves on the worst nights, though the only bell anyone can name sank with the old watch-tower two generations ago. The harbor-master has asked for outside help before the next spring tide, now less than a week away, brings whatever this is to a head.

## Setting

A weathered fishing town wrapped around a fog-choked bay. Rope-strung docks, gull-cry mornings, and a superstitious hush that falls over the taproom whenever someone mentions the water at night. The old watch-tower's stump still shows at the lowest tides, black and slick, like a rotten tooth.

## Suggested Genre & Lens

Fantasy. Works in any lens, but leans especially well toward **High Magic** (if you want the supernatural elements front and center) or **Sword & Sorcery** (for a grittier, more dangerous read on the same story).

## The Problem (What You're Told)

Three fishermen missing, all near or after high tide, all without a trace. A bell that shouldn't exist anymore is being heard beneath the water on bad nights. The harbor-master, Old Pell Ashgrove, is offering coin and lodging to anyone willing to find out what's happening before the spring tide — now six days off — makes things worse. The town is scared, and getting quieter about it, not louder.

## Suggested Drive & Bond Hooks

- **Drive:** *Find out what's taking the fishermen* — or, more personally, *Find [a specific missing fisherman] before it's too late.*
- **Drive:** *Protect Graybell Cove* — for a character already tied to the town.
- **Bond:** Old Pell Ashgrove, the harbor-master — scared, out of his depth, trying not to show it.
- **Bond:** A missing fisherman's sibling or partner, still holding out hope.

If you're bringing an existing character instead of building one for this hook, any Drive or Bond involving a missing person, an unsettled debt to the sea, or protecting a place they care about slots in naturally — the AI can help you fold it in during Session Zero.

## Tone & Safety Notes

Themes include drowning, disappearance, and a body of water that is not to be trusted. If loss-at-sea, drowning imagery, or missing-person themes are sensitive for you, raise it as a line or veil at Session Zero — the tone can flex without losing the hook.
