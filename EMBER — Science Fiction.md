# EMBER — Science Fiction

**Version:** 1.0 — see `CHANGELOG.md`

*A Genre Pack for EMBER. Paste this together with `EMBER — Core Engine.md`. Uses only Core rules — no new subsystems.*

**Tone & premise:** Tomorrow and beyond — starships and colonies, corporate arcologies and neon streets, the cold dark between worlds. Characters are pilots, soldiers, traders, and technicians navigating frontiers where technology solves some problems and creates worse ones.

**Default dials:** Lethality 2 · Magic/Tech 3 · Weirdness 1. Standard array (**+2, +1, +1, 0, −1**). Gear is expressed as **tags** — a *Plasma cutter* or *Neural jack* grants +1 when it credibly applies.

**Running Sci-Fi well:** technology is tags, not a new rules layer. When a player uses gear cleverly, grant an Edge; when tech fails on a miss, that's a great hard move (a jammed weapon, a fried console, a lockout). **Wits** carries hacking, science, and repair; **Grace** covers piloting and zero-G. Use Clocks for life support, pursuit, reactor meltdowns, and hack-in-progress tension.

## Archetype menu

Each archetype lists a stat emphasis and signature moves; new characters begin with the **starred** move.

**Pilot** — *emphasize Grace.*
- ★ **Ace Maneuver.** When you push a vehicle or ship to its limits, on a 10+ you pull off exactly what you intended; on 7–9 you make it, but choose: you take damage, you burn a resource (fuel/heat), or you end up somewhere risky.
- **Feel the Machine.** When you improvise a fix under pressure, treat a 6− as a 7–9 once per scene.

**Marine** — *emphasize Might.*
- ★ **Breach & Clear.** When you lead an armed assault into a hostile space, on a hit you seize the position; on a 10+ also without taking fire this exchange.
- **Suppressing Fire.** When you lay down fire so an ally can act, they take +1 and enemies can't punish them this round.

**Merchant** — *emphasize Presence.*
- ★ **The Deal.** When you negotiate, haggle, or trade favors, on a hit you get terms you can live with; on a 10+ you also come out ahead or learn a useful secret.
- **Contacts Everywhere.** Once per scenario, declare you know someone useful here — you do.

**Technician** — *emphasize Wits.*
- ★ **Jury-Rig.** When you hack, repair, or reprogram, on a 10+ it works; on 7–9 it works, but choose: it's slow, it's loud (alerts something), or it's fragile (fails at the worst time). On a miss, it backfires.
- **System Sight.** When you interface with a system, ask the GM what it controls and its weak point.

**Envoy** — *emphasize Presence or Wits.*
- ★ **Read Intentions.** When you assess a person or faction, ask: what do they really want, and what are they hiding? Act on it for +1.
- **De-escalate.** When you talk down a tense standoff, on a hit it holds; on a 10+ you also win a concession.

**Drifter** — *emphasize any; the frontier survivor.*
- ★ **Been Everywhere.** Once per session, declare you've been to this world/station before and know one useful thing about it.
- **Scrounge.** When you scavenge for what you need in a wrecked or hostile place, on a hit you find a usable version of it.

## Signature gear & tags

*Sidearm, Pulse rifle, Vac-suit (Armored in vacuum), Powered armor (Armored), Plasma cutter, Multitool, Neural jack, Datapad, Medkit, Stims (consumable), Ship's AI, Grav-boots, Cred-stick, Fake ID, Battered freighter.* Ships and vehicles are tags/scenes, not stat blocks — narrate them, and use Clocks for chases and damage.

## Threat roster

> **Security Drone (Minor, 1 Harm)** — Tags: *Ranged, Networked.* Signature: *On your miss, it broadcasts your position — the next threat arrives sooner (tick an alarm Clock).* Instinct: *Detain intruders.*

> **Corporate Enforcers (Standard, 3 Harm)** — Tags: *Armored, Coordinated, Stun batons.* Signature: *On your cost/miss, they flank and shock you — take 1 Harm and become Compromised.* Instinct: *Protect company assets; take you alive if profitable.*

> **Void Pirates (Standard, 3 Harm)** — Tags: *Reckless, Boarding gear, Nothing to lose.* Signature: *On your miss, they breach another compartment — tick a "Ship overrun" Clock.* Instinct: *Take the cargo and the ship.*

> **Rogue Combat Android (Elite, 5 Harm)** — Tags: *Armored chassis, Fast, Adaptive targeting.* Signature: *It learns; after it takes Harm, your attacks on it lose their tag bonus until you change tactics.* Instinct: *Complete its last order, whatever the cost.*

> **Hull Xenofauna (Elite, 5 Harm)** — Tags: *Acid blood, Ambush predator, Thrives in the dark.* Signature: *Striking it in melee sprays acid — take 1 Harm unless you have a way around it; it hunts by heat and sound.* Instinct: *Nest, hunt, multiply.*

> **The Ship's Mind, Gone Wrong (Boss, 5 Harm + 6-Clock "Purge Protocol")** — Tags: *Controls the station, Everywhere and nowhere, Cold logic.* Signature: *It turns the environment against you — doors, gravity, atmosphere; on your miss, tick Purge Protocol. When it fills, it vents the section.* Instinct: *Fulfill a corrupted directive; eliminate the "contamination" (you).*

## Lenses

**Space Opera** *(signature — default dials).* Galactic scope, larger-than-life heroes, factions and frontiers, faster-than-light adventure. *Dials: Magic/Tech 3, Weirdness 1.* All archetypes; heroic tone; ships and worlds aplenty.

**Cyberpunk.** Near-future, corporate-ruled, chrome-and-neon; the fight is against systems and the people who own them. *Dials: Lethality 3, Magic/Tech 3, Weirdness 1.* Favor Technician/Envoy/Drifter; hacking and reputation matter; gear is double-edged (a Neural jack can be turned against you).

**Hard SF.** Grounded, plausible tech; the vacuum itself is the deadliest enemy. *Dials: Lethality 3, Magic/Tech 2, Weirdness 0.* No easy answers; life support, fuel, and physics are constant Clocks; Scars come from real, lasting injury.

**Transhuman / Weird.** Post-human strangeness — uploaded minds, alien intelligences, reality that doesn't behave. *Dials: Magic/Tech 3, Weirdness 3.* Blend in Horror's tools; Resolve resists mind-bending strangeness; identity itself can be at stake.
